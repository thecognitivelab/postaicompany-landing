The post-AI company
doesn't exist yet.

We're documenting its birth. What changes when AI is not the product but the infrastructure? How do teams, workflows, org charts, and careers reorganize when intelligence is abundant and cheap? This is the question. We're here to answer it — with data, not speculation.

Thesis

AI is the new electricity.
But nobody's redesigning the factory.

Companies are adopting AI tools without rethinking the organization around them. That's like putting electric motors in a steam-powered factory and calling it innovation. The post-AI company isn't about adding AI — it's about reorganizing around what AI makes possible.

"The question isn't 'what can AI do?' — it's 'what should a company look like when intelligence costs zero?' Everything flows from that."
What Changes

Everything — starting with these six

01

Work hours dissolve

When AI handles async work 24/7, the 9-to-5 becomes optional. The question becomes: what's the minimum synchronous time a team actually needs?

02

Teams shrink, leverage explodes

A 3-person company with AI agents can do what took 30 people in 2024. Headcount stops being a proxy for capability. Output per person becomes the only metric that matters.

03

Management is rewritten

When AI handles task assignment, progress tracking, and performance data, the middle manager's job shifts from coordination to judgment. What's the new span of control?

04

Careers become portfolios

The 40-year single-track career dies. Post-AI workers manage a portfolio of projects, revenue streams, and learning loops. Loyalty shifts from company to capability.

05

Decision velocity is the moat

When everyone has access to the same AI, competitive advantage moves from information access to decision speed. The fastest decision-maker wins.

06

Culture becomes architecture

In a world of AI-mediated work, culture isn't soft — it's infrastructure. How a company decides, communicates, and resolves conflict becomes its actual operating system.

Early Signals

Companies already
operating in post-AI mode

They weren't born post-AI. But they're closer than anyone. We track them.

11 people, $200M revenue

Midjourney

The extreme outlier. ~11 employees, ~$200M annual revenue, $12.5M per person. No management hierarchy. AI-mediated coordination. Proves the upper bound of what's possible when you refuse to scale headcount.

20 people, $100M ARR

Cursor / Anysphere

$5M revenue per employee. AI-augmented development from day zero. No legacy processes to unwind. The default org chart for the post-AI era: tiny team, massive leverage.

$500M ARR, 5× revenue, +34% headcount

Perplexity

Went from $100M to $500M ARR while growing team only 34%. That leverage ratio — 5× revenue growth on 1.34× headcount — is the post-AI efficiency curve in action.

$400M ARR, no sales team

Lovable

146 employees, $2.7M per person. Adds ~1,500 paying customers per day without a traditional sales organization. AI handles lead gen, onboarding, and support. Humans handle product.

$500M ARR, 50% B2B

ElevenLabs

From $200M to $500M ARR in under a year. Half enterprise revenue — proving AI-native efficiency scales beyond PLG into Fortune 500. Voice AI as infrastructure, not gimmick.

$20M ARR, ~30 people

Bolt.new / StackBlitz

$2M per employee. Browser-based AI development environment. Entire product is AI-mediated — the tool builds itself. A glimpse of what happens when the product IS the post-AI workflow.
